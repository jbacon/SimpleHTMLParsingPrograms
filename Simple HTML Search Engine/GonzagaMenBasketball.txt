<p>The <b>Gonzaga Bulldogs men's basketball team</b> is the <a href="/wiki/College_basketball" title="College basketball">intercollegiate men's basketball</a> program representing <a href="/wiki/Gonzaga_University" title="Gonzaga University">Gonzaga University</a>. The school competes in the <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_Conference" title="West Coast Conference">West Coast Conference</a> in <a href="/wiki/Division_I_(NCAA)" title="Division I (NCAA)">Division I</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Collegiate_Athletic_Association" title="National Collegiate Athletic Association">National Collegiate Athletic Association</a> (NCAA). The <a href="/wiki/Gonzaga_Bulldogs" title="Gonzaga Bulldogs">Bulldogs</a> play home basketball games at the <a href="/wiki/McCarthey_Athletic_Center" title="McCarthey Athletic Center">McCarthey Athletic Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spokane,_Washington" title="Spokane, Washington">Spokane, Washington</a> on the university campus.</p>
<p>Gonzaga has had 12 of its players receive the <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_Conference_Men%27s_Basketball_Player_of_the_Year" title="West Coast Conference Men's Basketball Player of the Year">WCC Player of the Year</a> award in its history.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonzaga_Bulldogs_men%27s_basketball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Team history">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Team_history">Team history</span></h2>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonzaga_Bulldogs_men%27s_basketball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span></h3>
<p>Gonzaga introduced a basketball program during the 1907–08 basketball season. During that season, they had no coach, but managed to achieve a record of 9–2 (.818).<sup id="cite_ref-GB-Record1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GB-Record1-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> In the 1908/09 season, <a href="/wiki/George_Varnell" title="George Varnell">George Varnell</a> became the first official coach for Gonzaga, earning a 10–2 (.833) record during his only season with Gonzaga. Varnell was replaced by <a href="/wiki/William_Mulligan" title="William Mulligan" class="mw-redirect">William Mulligan</a> the following season, who acquired an 11–3 (.786) record.<sup id="cite_ref-MediaGuide3_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MediaGuide3-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Frank_McKevitt" title="Frank McKevitt">Frank McKevitt</a> took over for Mulligan during the 1910–11 basketball season, acquiring an 8–1 (.889) record, which was the highest winning percentage for Gonzaga basketball at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-MediaGuide3_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MediaGuide3-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonzaga_Bulldogs_men%27s_basketball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Dan Monson (1997–1999)">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Dan_Monson_.281997.E2.80.931999.29">Dan Monson (1997–1999)</span></h3>
<p>In 1997, Gonzaga assistant coach <a href="/wiki/Dan_Monson" title="Dan Monson">Dan Monson</a>, the son of veteran <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Ducks_men%27s_basketball" title="Oregon Ducks men's basketball">Oregon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Idaho_Vandals_men%27s_basketball" title="Idaho Vandals men's basketball">Idaho</a> basketball coach <a href="/wiki/Don_Monson" title="Don Monson">Don Monson</a>, became head coach of Gonzaga as Fitzgerald wanted to focus on his athletic director's duties.<sup id="cite_ref-Bol04_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bol04-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> During his first season, Monson led the Zags to a 24–10 record and a <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_Conference" title="West Coast Conference">WCC</a> regular-season title, which was not enough to land Gonzaga an at-large bid into the <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Men%27s_Division_I_Basketball_Championship" title="NCAA Men's Division I Basketball Championship">NCAA Tournament</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bol04_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bol04-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> However, the Bulldogs would earn a bid into the <a href="/wiki/1998_National_Invitation_Tournament" title="1998 National Invitation Tournament">1998 National Invitation Tournament</a>, where they beat <a href="/wiki/Wyoming_Cowboys_basketball" title="Wyoming Cowboys basketball">Wyoming</a> 69–55 in the first round before falling to <a href="/wiki/Hawaii_Rainbow_Warriors_basketball" title="Hawaii Rainbow Warriors basketball">Hawai'i</a> 78–70 in the second round.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>During the 1998–99 season, the Bulldogs finished with a 28–7 record, which was enough for Gonzaga to make it as a 10-seed into the <a href="/wiki/1999_NCAA_Men%27s_Division_I_Basketball_Tournament" title="1999 NCAA Men's Division I Basketball Tournament">1999 NCAA Tournament</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bra09_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bra09-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Gonzaga beat fifth-seeded <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Golden_Gophers_men%27s_basketball" title="Minnesota Golden Gophers men's basketball">Minnesota</a> 75–63 in the first round and followed it with an 82–74 win over second-seeded <a href="/wiki/Stanford_Cardinal_men%27s_basketball" title="Stanford Cardinal men's basketball">Stanford</a> to advance to the regional semifinals.<sup id="cite_ref-1999_NCAA_Tournament_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1999_NCAA_Tournament-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> The Zags would go on to beat <a href="/wiki/Florida_Gators_men%27s_basketball" title="Florida Gators men's basketball">Florida</a> 73–72 to advance to the regional finals after <a href="/wiki/Casey_Calvary" title="Casey Calvary">Casey Calvary</a> tipped in the winning basket with four seconds remaining.<sup id="cite_ref-Bol04_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bol04-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> They trailed eventual national champion <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_Huskies_men%27s_basketball" title="Connecticut Huskies men's basketball">UConn</a> by one point with a minute remaining before losing 67–62 in the regional finals.<sup id="cite_ref-Elite_Eight_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Elite_Eight-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonzaga_Bulldogs_men%27s_basketball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mark Few (1999–present)">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Mark_Few_.281999.E2.80.93present.29">Mark Few (1999–present)</span></h3>
<p>Exposure from Gonzaga's elite eight run in the NCAA Tournament caused Monson to take a head coaching position at <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Golden_Gophers" title="Minnesota Golden Gophers">Minnesota</a>, at which time Gonzaga assistant coach <a href="/wiki/Mark_Few" title="Mark Few">Mark Few</a> became the head coach in the <a href="/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_NCAA_Division_I_men%27s_basketball_season" title="1999–2000 NCAA Division I men's basketball season">1999–00 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bol04_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bol04-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Monson_Leaves_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Monson_Leaves-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonzaga_Bulldogs_men%27s_basketball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: McCarthey Athletic Center">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="McCarthey_Athletic_Center">McCarthey Athletic Center</span></h2>
<p>Gonzaga home games have been played at the <a href="/wiki/McCarthey_Athletic_Center" title="McCarthey Athletic Center">McCarthey Athletic Center</a> since 2004. The Bulldogs opened the arena with a 38-game winning streak, the longest in the NCAA at the time. The streak ended in February 2007 with a loss to the <a href="/wiki/Santa_Clara_Broncos" title="Santa Clara Broncos">Santa Clara Broncos</a>. When combined with 12 wins at home in the old <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Y._Martin_Centre" title="Charlotte Y. Martin Centre">Charlotte Y. Martin Centre</a> "Kennel", the overall home-game winning streak ended at 50 games.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Between 1999 and 2009, Gonzaga compiled a 120–9 record at home, and a 33–2 record in conference. As of 10 December 2011<span style="display:none">&#160;(<span class="bday dtstart published updated">2011 -12-10</span>)</span><sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gonzaga_Bulldogs_men%27s_basketball&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the team's record for all games played in the McCarthey Athletic Center was 92-7.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonzaga_Bulldogs_men%27s_basketball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The State Farm Battle in Seattle">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="The_State_Farm_Battle_in_Seattle">The State Farm Battle in Seattle</span></h2>

<p>The <b>State Farm Battle in Seattle</b> is the annual game that the Bulldogs play at <a href="/wiki/KeyArena" title="KeyArena">KeyArena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>, Washington. It is considered a neutral site game, considering that Seattle is about 300 miles (480&#160;km) from Gonzaga's hometown of Spokane, but it is essentially a home game for Gonzaga due to a typically heavy fan turnout. The first State Farm Battle in Seattle was in 2003.</p>
<p>Gonzaga is 5–4 in State Farm Battle in Seattle games, winning their first three (2003–2005), losing from 2006–2008, and then rotated wins and losses from 2009-2011, winning in 2009 and 2011, but losing in 2010.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonzaga_Bulldogs_men%27s_basketball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Impact on the University">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_on_the_University">Impact on the University</span></h2>
<p>Gonzaga University has experienced an inflow of students since the men's basketball team's 1998–99 season brought the school national attention. A 65-percent increase in the size of the freshman class between 1997 and 2003 is part of a phenomenon called the <a href="/wiki/Flutie_Effect" title="Flutie Effect">Flutie Effect</a>, the increase in attention and applications for admission that results after a particularly notable and unexpected sporting victory by a school's athletic team. Gonzaga University president Rev. Robert Spitzer said that the team's success was responsible for the school receiving the $23 million required to build the McCarthey Athletic Center, most of which was received through major gifts.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonzaga_Bulldogs_men%27s_basketball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Gonzaga vs. the AP Top 25 (since 1998–99)">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Gonzaga_vs._the_AP_Top_25_.28since_1998.E2.80.9399.29">Gonzaga vs. the AP Top 25 (since 1998–99)</span></h2>
<p>Since the season of Gonzaga's 1999 NCAA Division 1 Men's Basketball Tournament run to the Elite 8, Gonzaga has played a total of 63 games against teams ranked in the AP Top 25 Poll. Gonzaga has a record of 22–41 against such teams. They have beaten a team ranked #3 on three occasions (2003-04 season against Missouri, and the 2004-05 season against Georgia Tech and Oklahoma State), and beat a 2nd ranked North Carolina in November 2006.</p>
