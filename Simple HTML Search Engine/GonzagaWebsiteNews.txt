<p><strong>PULLMAN, Wash. (AP) -</strong> Kevin Pangos had a tough shooting night, until the game was on the line.</p>
<p>Then the Gonzaga guard drove the length of the court and made a layup with 2 seconds left for a 71-69 victory over Washington State on Wednesday night, giving the Bulldogs (9-0) the best start in their Division I history.</p>
<p>Pangos made just 1 of 11 shots before the game-winner.</p>
<p>``My thought process was to just get to the rim,'' Pangos said.</p>
<p>``It wasn't my best shooting night, to say the least,'' he said. ``But I've struggled offensively before. I forgot about the misses and just kept shooting.''</p>
<p>Elias Harris scored 23 points and Kelly Olynyk had 22, all in the second half, for Gonzaga, which had been tied with the 1994-95 team for best start.</p>
<p>``We gave game balls to both of these guys,'' Gonzaga coach Mark Few said of Harris and Olynyk. ``These two players down the stretch were big for us.''</p>
<p>Olynyk, who redshirted last season, made 10 of 14 shots to keep Gonzaga in the game in the second half.</p>
<p>``Olynyk couldn't have made these plays a year ago,'' Few said. ``He's turning into a strong player.''</p>
<p>Brock Motum had 23 points and DaVonte Lacy added 22 for Washington State (5-4), which saw its three-game winning streak snapped.</p>
<p>Motum hit a 3-pointer with 14 seconds left to bring WSU within 68-67.</p>
<p>Gary Bell Jr. made a free throw for Gonzaga, and Lacy made a layup to tie the score at 69.</p>
<p>That set up Pangos' drive that gave him eight points.</p>
<p>``It was a great college basketball game,'' Washington State coach Ken Bone said. ``It was a fun game to be involved in, a stressful game to coach.</p>
<p>``Gonzaga is very good,'' Bone said. ``There is a reason they are the No. 10 team in the country.''</p>
<p>Olynyk, who had 10 points in the first 8 minutes of the second half, scored three straight baskets as Gonzaga built a 41-36 lead.</p>
<p>Consecutive 3-pointers by Harris and Mike Hart gave Gonzaga a 56-45 lead with 7:26 left.</p>
<p>Lacy hit two 3-pointers to bring WSU within 58-51 with 6 minutes left and consecutive 3-pointers by Motum cut Gonzaga's lead to 60-57 with 3:31 left.</p>
<p>Lacy's 3-pointer tied the score at 60, but Olynyk made a putback and the free throw for a 63-60 lead. Two free throws by Motum cut Gonzaga's lead to one.</p>
<p>Lacy was fouled on a drive and made both free throws to put WSU up 64-63 with 1:29 left. Olynyk made a layup for a 65-64 lead. He missed the free throw, but Gonzaga got the rebound and Olynyk hit a 3-pointer for a 68-64 lead with 33.5 seconds left.</p>
<p>``This was a very enthusiastic effort down the stretch,'' Few said of his team.</p>
<p>Bone praised Lacy, who scored 17 points in the second half.</p>
<p>``There is a level of toughness and confidence and the other guys feed off his confidence,'' Bone said of Lacy. ``If he is on the floor, it raises our team's confidence.''</p>
<p>Washington State leads the all-time series between the eastern Washington rivals 98-49.</p>
