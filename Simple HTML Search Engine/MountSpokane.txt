			<h1>
	Mt. Spokane State Park</h1>
<p>
	<img alt="" src="/core/files/mtspokane/uploads/images/parksignage.gif" style="width: 442px; height: 330px; float: right; margin-left: 10px; margin-right: 10px;" />Mt. Spokane Ski &amp; Snowboard Park operates within <a href="http://parks.wa.gov/parks/?selectedpark=Mount%20Spokane&amp;subject=all" target="_blank">Mt. Spokane State Park,</a> the largest of Washington&#39;s State Parks at 13,919 acres.&nbsp; In addition to alpine winter recreation, the Park offers year-round recreation including nordic skiing, snowshoeing, snowmobiling, camping, horseback riding, biking and hiking trails, berry picking, sightseeing, and more. &nbsp;</p>
<p>
	NEWS: The Discover Pass is now required within Mt. Spokane State Park, excluding the alpine ski area parking lot when the alpine ski area is in normal operation.<br />
	<a href="http://www.discoverpass.wa.gov/" target="_blank">Click for information</a></p>
<p>
	<a href="/core/files/mtspokane/uploads/files/MSSPGeneral.pdf">Click here for a Mt. Spokane State Park Map</a></p>
<h3>
	Nordic Ski Area</h3>
<p>
	The Park features 25 kilometers of beautifully groomed nordic ski trails that are multi-use in the spring, summer, and fall and for cross-country skiing in the winter.&nbsp; The Park annually hosts the legendary <a href="http://spokanelanglauf.org/" target="_blank">Langlauf cross-country ski race</a>.&nbsp; The trails are groomed regularly, excluding Mondays &amp; Tuesdays.&nbsp; Call the Nordic conditions line at (509) 238-4025 for the most up-to-date weather and grooming information.&nbsp; A Sno-Park permit is required for parking in designated nordic and snowmobile lots from November 1 through May1.&nbsp; A groomed trails permit is additionally required for usage of the groomed trails.&nbsp; Click here to download a nordic trail map, courtesy Spokane Nordic and Tom Frost.&nbsp; See <a href="http://www.spokanenordic.org/" target="_blank">Spokane Nordic</a> for information on children&#39;s programs, races, and more.</p>
<h3>
	Snowmobiling</h3>
<p>
	Snowmobiling is allowed within the Park on designated trails.&nbsp; A Sno-Park permit is required for parking in designated nordic and snowmobile lots from November 1 through May1.&nbsp; A groomed trails permit is additionally required for usage of the groomed trails.&nbsp; <a href="/core/files/mtspokane/uploads/files/MtSpokaneSnowmobileMap.pdf">Download an unofficial snowmobile trails map</a>, courtesy Friends of Mt. Spokane.</p>
<h3>
	Camping</h3>
<p>
	The State Park has 8 standard campsites, as well as a larger group campsite.&nbsp; The campground is generally open from mid-May through mid-September.&nbsp; <a href="http://parks.wa.gov/parks/?selectedpark=Mount%20Spokane&amp;subject=all" target="_blank">See Parks website for pricing and availability details</a>.</p>
<h3>
	Drive to the Top</h3>
<p>
	The Summit Road is generally open from mid-June through mid-October, and is open all the way to the summit of the mountain, the highest point in Spokane County.&nbsp; Enjoy spectacular vistas and wildlife on the road to the summit.&nbsp; Once on top, visit the historic <a href="/lodges">Vista House</a>, as well as other monuments of historical significance.&nbsp; View area lakes with the telescopic viewfinders.&nbsp; Restrooms are available on the summit.</p>
